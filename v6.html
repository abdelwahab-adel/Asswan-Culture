information about Unfinished Obelisk

The Unfinished Obelisk in Aswan is considered one of the most important archaeological landmarks in Egypt, showcasing the advanced skills of the ancient Egyptians in stone carving and the construction of massive obelisks. It is located in an ancient quarry on the hill of Stela in Aswan and would have been the largest obelisk in the world had it been completed, but the work was halted due to a crack that appeared in the granite during carving. The obelisk measures approximately 42 meters in length and is estimated to weigh around 1,200 tons, making it one of the greatest engineering attempts of the Pharaonic era. The Unfinished Obelisk demonstrates the precise techniques used by the ancient craftsmen for extracting and carving stone and serves as an important resource for studying the methods and tools employed in obelisk construction. Today, the Unfinished Obelisk attracts tourists and researchers from around the world and stands as a symbol of the strength and grandeur of ancient Egyptian civilization, illustrating their ability to undertake monumental engineering projects despite limited technology.
