The most famous foods in Aswan

Aswan is famous for its many authentic, popular, and distinctive Nubian and Upper Egyptian dishes, which rely on simplicity and local ingredients.

alsalat

One of the most famous Nubian dishes is to put the meat in a pot on the fire with pepper and onion and stir until it softens, then add ghee and stir until it browns, then add water and leave it on the fire until it is cooked.

Jakoud

The dish known as "Jakoud" is an integral part of Nubian heritage, served at celebrations, weddings, and during Ramadan. Also known as "Itr," it's a popular traditional Nubian dish similar to molokhia, but prepared in a unique way that differs from the usual method, giving it a distinctive taste and authentic, heritage-inspired flavor that reflects Nubian cuisine.

Abreh

Abreh is a distinctive Nubian drink, renowned for its thirst-quenching properties, and is prepared especially during Ramadan. Made from natural ingredients and served chilled, it is a popular staple on the Nubian iftar table, particularly during hot weather.

Asida Aswan

Asida is one of the most famous traditional dishes of Aswan, made from flour, milk, water, and salt. It is considered a staple on the breakfast table in Aswan, served hot, and is known for its nutritional value and ease of preparation.

Khamarit

Khamarit is a popular traditional dish in Aswan and Upper Egypt. It is made from flour, yeast, and sugar, and is either baked or pan-fried until golden brown. Khamarit is characterized by its sweet taste and is often served for breakfast or as a light dessert, especially on special occasions.

Karmdid

Karmdid is a popular dish in Aswan, especially during the winter months, due to its high nutritional value and warming properties. It is prepared using ground fenugreek, oil, salt, flour, and water, and cooked until it reaches a thick consistency and a distinctive flavor.

Aswan liver(kabdat)

It is served at celebrations and weddings. This dish has a special place in most Aswan homes, where the hot ingredients are prepared on-site, giving it a unique and distinctive flavor.

Maltout

Maltout (Sun Bread): Maltout, or sun bread, is one of the oldest and most famous types of bread in Upper Egypt. It is made from wheat flour and left to ferment in the sun before being baked in a traditional oven or tabouna. Maltout is a staple on the Upper Egyptian table and is closely linked to heritage and ancient customs.
